About Danfe Hotel & Restaurant

A family-run establishment serving trekkers for over a decade

Our Story

Named after Nepal's magnificent national bird, the Danfe (Himalayan Monal), our hotel has been a welcoming stop for trekkers on the Annapurna Circuit since its establishment. We understand the needs of mountain adventurers because we are mountain people ourselves.

Our family has called Chame home for generations, and we take pride in sharing the warmth and hospitality of our Himalayan culture with visitors from around the world. Every meal we serve, every warm bed we provide, and every story we share comes from our deep love for these mountains and the people who journey through them.

Why Chame?

Chame, the district headquarters of Manang, sits at an elevation of 2,670 meters (8,760 feet) and serves as a crucial stop on the Annapurna Circuit. The town offers spectacular views of Annapurna II, and is often where trekkers take their first acclimatization day.
